About Me

I started my high school and college career focused on engineering. I joined the FRC robotics team at my high school and went into university majoring in Computer Engineering. After joining Clarkson Club Television, the production team behind the sports broadcasts at Clarkson, I realized I had a new passion. I took a leap of faith and ended up in my new major. I now am the woman behind the cameras, affectionately named “the blonde in the booth” by my mother. I am in charge of the stream output to ESPN+ with over 9,000 average viewers. I direct camera operators, replay operatives, radio broadcasters, and the graphics operative. Our production team broadcasted the ECAC Hockey Men’s Championship games in Lake Placid 2022 and 2023 to ESPN+.

I might have moved on, the roots of engineering still lay at my foundation. I continue to enjoy coding, using HTML and CSS to create and design websites. I created an application using Adobe XD for the school newspaper and I am currently working on the corresponding website.

UX Prototype

The Clarkson Integrator was once a physical newspaper that reflected the mindset of students on the Clarkson University campus. The former Clarkson Integrator never changed from using physical copies into the digital age and lost many readers because of it. After disbanding a couple years ago, I decided to create a prototype of the newspaper in order to bring it back. The prototype I constructed is an application in which the user is able to read, comment, and share articles written by college students on the Clarkson University campus.
