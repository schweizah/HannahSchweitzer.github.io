About Me

I started my high school and college career focused on engineering. I joined the FRC robotics team at my high school and went into university majoring in Computer Engineering. After joining Clarkson Club Television, the production team behind the sports broadcasts at Clarkson, I realized I had a new passion. I took a leap of faith and ended up in my new major. I now am the woman behind the cameras, affectionately named “the blonde in the booth” by my mother. I am in charge of the stream output to ESPN+ with over 9,000 average viewers. I direct camera operators, replay operatives, radio broadcasters, and the graphics operative. Our production team broadcasted the ECAC Hockey Men’s Championship games in Lake Placid 2022 and 2023 to ESPN+.

While I might have moved on, the roots of engineering still lay at my foundation. I continue to enjoy coding, using HTML and CSS to create and design websites. I created an application using Adobe XD for the school newspaper and I am currently working on the corresponding website.

Clarkson Club Television (CCTV)

Clarkson Club Television (CCTv) is the main broadcasting hub for Clarkson University sports and events. While hockey is our main production, we also broadcast soccer, volleyball, swimming, basketball, lacrosse, baseball, and softball. In addition to athletics, we also stream on campus happenings at Clarkson such as: graduation events, recognition events, robotics tournaments, and TED talks. We teach students live broadcasting skills and give them opportunities to gain professional experience. Our club is essential for parents, alumni, and prospective students to view sports and events on campus. Due to our high quality productions, we were asked by the ECAC organization to broadcast the ECAC Hockey Championships at Lake Placid in 2022 and 2023. We hope that our organization as whole can continue to be a valuable media asset to the Clarkson University campus.

I am the current president and former business manager of Clarkson Club Television. I plan meetings, delegate tasks, and schedules crew members. I train new members on equipment and job etiquette. I run a social media campaign to garner more interest in membership with views over 50k. I designed a new studio for club needs. I collaborate with ECAC hockey league to make sure the broadcast is being run up to standard. I am in charge of purchasing needed equipment and writing budget request forms and manage the financial transactions of a club with over $100,000 in deprecation.

UX Prototype

The Clarkson Integrator was once a physical newspaper that reflected the mindset of students on the Clarkson University campus. The former Clarkson Integrator never changed from using physical copies into the digital age and lost many readers because of it. After disbanding a couple years ago, I decided to create a prototype of the newspaper in order to bring it back. The prototype I constructed is an application in which the user is able to read, comment, and share articles written by college students on the Clarkson University campus.
